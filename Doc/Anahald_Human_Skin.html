Observations from the above comparison - the author's take:

  1. Differences between the conversions are less pronounceable when viewed through red-cyan glasses.
  2. StereoPhotoMaker-"Dubois" and undocumented StereoPhotoMaker-"SPM" options are off.
  3. StereoPhotoMaker-"Half-color" option appears fine at the first glance because the face looks good; but do notice the shoulder becoming wax-colored.
  4. Undocumented StereoPhotoMaker-"Suto" option looks good.
  5. Anahald-"Color-Preserve", Anahald-"Moderate-Correction", and to some degree Anahald-"Eye-Comfort" look good also.
  6. Anahald-"Color-Preserve" is nearly indistinguishable from StereoPhotoMaker-"Suto".

Now let's put all the usable options side-by-side for the final glance. This time Anahald-originated conversions are made darker for more fare comparison with the StereoPhotoMaker-"Half-color" variant.

To be honest, the model's skin has a very similar appearance on all the four conversions when viewing through the author's DIY dual-film-filter glasses.
With "Nvidia 3d-vision discover" glasses, the advantage of Anahald-based options is subtle, though undoubtedly apparent.
